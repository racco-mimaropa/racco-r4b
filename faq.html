Application Phase

If you are a childless couple wanting to adopt through Regular Adoption process, the average duration from the submission up to the approval of application is 1-2 months, provided that there are no additional documents that will be requested by the RACCO.

However, for Independent Placement cases who already has custody of the child before the effectivity of the law, you need to wait for the child to be declared as legally available for adoption first, prior the application as adoptive parents.


Matching Process

The waiting time to be matched to a child will depend as to your child preference (age, sex, health condition, etc.) Typically, the younger your child preference is, the longer the waiting period to be matched. This is because all children need to go through the process of being declared as legally available for adoption before they can be adopted. This process is needed to ensure that a child to be matched has no longer legal ties with the birth parent/ relative.

Matching Process may also apply with relative adoption who has less 2 years of custody of the adoptee.


Petition phase

The duration process from the time of filing of petition up to finality of adoption may take an average of 1 year. Of course, the process may take much faster if the documents are found to be complete in form and substance.

For adoption cases whose category falls under Relative, Step-parent and Adult Adoption, they will go straight to the filing of petition.

Foreign Prospective Adoptive Parents should get first a certified true copy of their country or state’s law on domestic adoption from their government’s official gazette/ publication. Some countries have official website where a certified true copy of the law can be downloaded. Should you find this difficult to obtain, you may consult a lawyer in the Philippines or in their country, whichever is convenient for you.

Once a certified true copy of the law is obtained, this should be authenticated and apostilled in their country’s department of foreign affairs.

If the foreign PAPs’ country is a signatory of Hague Apostille Convention, it should be apostilled. If not, it should be authenticated.

There are no exceptions. All foreign PAP/s should exert their earnest efforts in procuring the same.
