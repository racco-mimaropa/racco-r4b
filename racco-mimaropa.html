APPLICATION AS ADOPTIVE PARENTS

Are you a childless couple or interested in legal adoption of a child not your blood? You may apply as Prospective Adoptive Parents for you to be matched to a child

Things to consider before adopting:

  1. READINESS TO ADOPT. Adoption is permanent. This means that once you go through this process, you may not return a child to his/ her biological family. Furthermore, each child for adoption has unique history. They may come from a family with history of abuse, drug use, disability, mental health issues, or if the child is abandoned, family history may be not known. Hence, you need to be prepared in addressing possible challenges in parenting an adopted child.
  2. COST OF ADOPTION. Through administrative adoption, the adoption process was made simpler and less costly. However, this does not mean you will not incur expenses. You still need to prepare financially especially in obtaining the necessary documents
  3. AGE OF THE CHILD. In Philippine laws, a child needs to be declared as legally available for adoption first, before they can be matched to an adoptive family. This process may take from months to years. Hence, if you want to adopt an infant or younger child, the waiting time to be matched to a younger child may be longer compared when you adopt a much older child.
REQUIREMENTS

STEPS IN APPLYING AS PROSPECTIVE ADOPTIVE PARENTS

  1. Attend a pre-adoption forum.
  2. Seek assistance of an adoption social worker. An adoption social worker may come from your Local Social Welfare and Development Office (LSWDO) where you reside or respective Regional Alternative Child Care Office (RACCO)
  3. Submit a Letter of intent on adoption.
  4. If initially assessed to be qualified, complete the required documents.
  5. Undergo interviews and assessments. Your social worker may conduct in-person or telephone interviews to you, your family and other relevant persons.
  6. Submit application with complete supporting documents to RACCO.
  7. Appear to the Regional Child Placement Committee (RCPC). RACCO will send an invitation letter for you to appear before the RCPC. The RCPC is a group of specialists who will interview you and deliberate whether to approve or deny your application as Prospective Adoptive Parent. This may be done via online or in-person.
  8. Wait for the results of the application. If approved, you will receive a communication and will be included in the roster of approved Prospective Adoptive Parents
  9. Wait to be matched to a child within your preference.
  10. Once matched, a communication will be sent to you pertaining to the results and child matched to you. Prospective Adoptive Parent/s will be given time to decide to accept or not the results of the matching.
  11. Send your letter of acceptance or non-acceptance of the results.
  12. Go to the Child Caring Agency for the physical entrustment of the child. If you accepted, a schedule will be set for the placement of the child with you. You will need to go directly where the child is. Some Child Caring Agency may require you to spend 1-2 nights in the agency or around the area to help the child adjust before he/she be placed/ entrusted with you.
  13. Complete six (6) months of Supervised Trial Custody. After the placement, your adoption social worker will oversee the adjustment between you and the child. After thorough assessment of satisfactory adjustment where bond and rapport have taken place, your social worker will recommend for you to proceed with filing a petition for administrative adoption.

The application with complete supporting documents must be submitted in two sets (one original and one photocopy) at the RACCO of the Region where the PAP/s reside. The manner of submission may be through in person or through courier.

For the guidance of adoptive applicant/s, they must attend a pre-adoption first.



FILING A PETITION FOR ADMINISTRATIVE ADOPTION

The filing of petition for adoption was before done in court. However, with the passage of Republic Act 11642 last 2022, adopters no need to hire a lawyer nor go through the judicial process of adoption.

Prospective Adoptive Parents (PAPs) who would like to adopt a child who is their relative or step-child will go straight in Filing a Petition for Administrative Adoption. The same goes to people who would like to adopt an adult person that they considered their own since he/she was still a minor.

For PAPs who went through the application process and was matched to a child legally available for adoption, you will need to file a petition in order to legalize and finalize the adoption of the child.

QUALIFICATIONS

Any Filipino citizens or foreign national who:

  1. Is at least 25 years old and have 16 years age difference with the adoptee (may be waived if the adopter is the child’s biological parent/s or spouse of the child’s parent.)
  2. Is in possession of full civil capacity and legal rights
  3. Has not been convicted of any crime involving moral turpitude
  4. Is of good moral character and can model the same
  5. Is in the position to support and care for the child in keeping with the means of the family

Additional qualification for Foreign nationals:

  • Permanent or habitual residents of the Philippines for 5 years. The residency requirement may be waived if the foreign national is:

    - A former Filipino habitually residing in the Philippines who seeks to adopt a relative within 4th degree

    - A Foreigner who seeks to adopt the marital child of the Filipino spouse

    - A foreigner married to a Filipino and will jointly adopt the Filipino spouse’s relative within 4th degree

  • Came from a country whose Philippines have a diplomatic relation
  • Present a duly authenticated copy of foreign domestic law on adoption or Certification from relevant Central Authority that the NACC Order of Adoption is Hague Compliant

STEPS PRIOR THE FILING OF PETITION

For relative, step-parent and adult adoption:

  1. Attend a pre-adoption forum
  2. Avail the services of an adoption social worker. An adoption social worker may come from your Local Social Welfare and Development Office (LSWDO) where you reside or respective Regional Alternative Child Care Office (RACCO)
  3. Submit a Letter of intent on adoption.
  4. If assessed to be qualified, obtain the required documents
  5. Undergo assessment and interviews to be conducted by the adoption social worker
  6. Prepare petition with supporting documents

For regular prospective adoptive parents adopting a child legally available for adoption:

  1. All your original documents submitted to RACCO will be returned in order for you to re-use them in the filing of petition.
  2. Update expired documents and ask for your adoption social worker to give an updated Social Case Study Report
  3. Prepare petition with supporting documents

FILING OF PETITION

  1. File or submit your Notarized Petition for Administrative Adoption along with complete supporting documents to Regional Alternative Child Care Office (RACCO). This may done through mail/ in-person submission
  2. Wait for acknowledgement or comments from RACCO.
  3. If found complete in documents, RACCO will will issue an Order informing you of the schedule and place of Mandatory Appearance and directing you to publish the said Order in a newspaper of general circulation once a week for three (3) consecutive weeks
  4. Secure Affidavit of Publication from the newspaper company and copies of the newspaper. Submit to RACCO. Submission may be done through courier to in-person during the Mandatory Appearance.
  5. Attend the scheduled Mandatory Appearance at RACCO. The RACCO Head will interview you and review the merits of the petition you have filed. After which, they will facilitate the endorsement of your petition to National Authority for Child (NACC)- Central Office and provide their recommendation to NACC's Executive Director whether to grant or deny the petition.
  6. If NACC-Central Office found the petition not sufficient or complete, they will issue a written communication to RACCO. You may need to comply and submit the lacking documents
  7. NACC Executive Director to sign decision and issue Order of Adoption or Denial. The said order will be transmitted via email of RACCO, PAPs and concerned Local Civil Registrar.
  8. Prepare acknowledgement letter of the receipt of the said Order and send via email of NACC copy furnishing the RACCO.
  9. After 15 working days, and no Motion for Reconsideration is filed, NACC Executive Director to issue Certificate of Finality
  10. Physical copies of the Order of Adoption and Certificate of Finality will be transmitted to you by the RACCO
  11. Once received, facilitate the registration of the Order of Adoption in the concerned Local Civil Registry Office where the child was born/ found.
  12. One (1) year post adoption monitoring to be conducted by adoption social worker. Case will be closed after a closing summary report has been submitted by the adoption social worker to RACCO.

WHERE TO FILE

PAPs must file the petition with complete supporting documents in two sets (one original and one photocopy) to the Regional Alternative Child Care Office where they reside. The manner of filing of petition may be through in person or through courier.

For the guidance of Prospective Adoptive Parent/s, they must attend a pre-adoption first.


SIMULATED BIRTH RECTIFICATION ACT (SIBRA)

Simulation of birth is an act of tampering the civil registry of a child making it appear in the birth records that he/she was born to a person who is not his/her biological mother, causing such child/person to lose his/her true identity and status. This action is punishable under Article 347 of the Revised Penal Code and Republic Act 11642.

However, with the passage of the Republic Act 11222, as amended by the Republic Act 11642, person/s who simulated a child's birth was given the opportunity to come out in the open and correct the birth records of the child under their care without fear of being prosecuted either criminally, civilly or administratively as the law grants amnesty, provided they will file the necessary petition. By availing of the privilege granted by the law, the existing parent-child relationship between them will be legalized.

  10. Child/ Adoptee has been in their custody since March 29, 2016 or earlier

• Permanent or habitual residents of the Philippines for 3 years prior the filing of petition for adoption under RA 11222. The residency requirement may be waived if the foreign national is:

- A Foreigner who seeks to adopt the marital child of the Filipino spouse

STEPS IN FILING A PETITION FOR RECTIFICATION OF SIMULATED BIRTH:

  1. Attend a pre-adoption forum. If found qualified under the law, proceed with the next step.
  2. Secure and complete documentary requirements. Prepare petition and facilitate notarization thereof.
  3. File the petition with complete supporting documents before the Local Social Welfare and Development Office (LSWDO) which has jurisdiction where the child resides.
  4. Pay necessary socialized fees at the Local Government Unit.
  5. LSWDO review and transmit petition with complete documents to Regional Alternative Child Care Office (RACCO)
  6. RACCO will acknowledgement and review your petition. Once found complete in documents, a RACCO social worker will coordinate with you for in-person assessment and interview. He/ She will prepare a Social Case Study Report
  7. An in-person Mandatory Appearance to RACCO Head will be scheduled.
  8. Attend the scheduled Mandatory Appearance at RACCO. The RACCO Head will interview you and review the merits of the petition you have filed. After which, they will facilitate the endorsement of your petition to National Authority for Child (NACC)- Central Office and provide their recommendation to NACC's Executive Director whether to grant or deny the petition.
  9. If NACC-Central Office found the petition not sufficient or complete, they will issue a written communication to RACCO. You may need to comply and submit the lacking documents
  10. NACC Executive Director to sign decision and issue Order of Adoption or Denial. The said order will be transmitted via email of RACCO, PAPs and concerned Local Civil Registrar.
  11. Prepare acknowledgement letter of the receipt of the said Order and send via email of NACC copy furnishing the RACCO.
  12. After 15 working days, and no Motion for Reconsideration is filed, NACC Executive Director to issue Certificate of Finality
  13. Physical copies of the Order of Adoption and Certificate of Finality will be transmitted to you by the RACCO
  14. Once received, facilitate the registration of the Order of Adoption in the concerned Local Civil Registry Office where the child was born/ found.
  15. One (1) year post adoption monitoring to be conducted by adoption social worker. Case will be closed after a closing summary report has been submitted by the adoption social worker to RACCO.

WHERE TO FILE

PAPs must file the petition with complete supporting documents in two sets (one original and one photocopy) to the Local Social Welfare and Development Office where the child/ adoptee lives regardless where the simulated birth record was registered.

For the guidance of Prospective Adoptive Parent/s, they must attend a pre-adoption first.
